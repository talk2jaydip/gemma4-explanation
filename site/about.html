About this explainer

Every architectural claim in this guide is anchored to a public source - Google's Gemma 4 model card, Google's vision docs, the Hugging Face Transformers docs, the public 31B config, the Keras Hub Gemma4Backbone, or foundational papers.

Where the public record is ambiguous (for example, whether internal training recipes match the public config byte-for-byte), the article says so explicitly. The goal is an honest, source-backed mental model - not a marketing summary and not a reverse-engineering claim.

What the numbers mean

The KV-cache estimate in section 8 is a teaching calculation. It uses public 31B config values (60 layers, 32 Q heads, 16 local KV heads, 4 global KV heads, 256 local head_dim, 512 global head_dim), BF16, batch 1, and assumes a compact rolling buffer for sliding layers. Real engines differ because of paged attention, quantized KV, padding, batching, and allocator overhead. Your production numbers will vary - the point is the ratio, not the exact GiB.

If you find an error: please file an issue at the project repo or reply on the Substack post. I'd rather update this and be right than leave a stale number up.
